Template Approval

How to Write Real Estate WhatsApp Templates That Are Easier to Review

A practical guide to template names, variables, categories, samples, buttons and responsible wording.

24 July 20269 min readJitendra KumarUpdated for practical planning

On this page

Start with the right expectation

A WhatsApp Business template is reviewed in context. Clear purpose, accurate category selection, readable variables and useful buttons improve preparation, but no wording can guarantee approval. Platform rules and review outcomes can change.

Treat every template as a precise customer communication—not as a place for exaggerated sales language.

Use a clear template name

Use lowercase letters, numbers and underscores. The name should describe the customer action and version.

real_estate_utility_site_visit_confirmation_v1

Choose the category from the actual purpose

Utility

Use utility wording when the message directly follows a customer request or an existing transaction, appointment or service process.

Marketing

Use marketing wording for launches, offers, reactivation, events and promotional updates. Include consent and an appropriate opt-out route.

Important: Meta may reclassify a template. Describe the real purpose honestly instead of trying to force a preferred category.

Keep variables understandable

Write with named variables while planning, then convert them to sequential Meta placeholders for submission.

Planning body:
Hi {{CustomerName}}, your visit to {{ProjectName}} is confirmed.

Meta body:
Hi {{1}}, your visit to {{2}} is confirmed.

Provide realistic samples for every variable. Avoid placing a variable by itself on a line.

Add buttons that match the message

Quick Reply buttons are best for a response and next-message branch. URL and phone CTA buttons open an external destination and do not send a customer reply.

  • Quick Reply: Confirm Visit
  • Quick Reply: Reschedule
  • URL CTA: Open Location
  • Phone CTA: Call Consultant

A review-ready example

Template: real_estate_utility_site_visit_confirmation_v1
Category: Utility
Language: English

Hi {{1}},

Your visit to {{2}} is scheduled for {{3}} at {{4}}.

Please confirm whether you will attend.

Quick Replies:
- Confirm Visit
- Reschedule
- Cancel Visit

CTA:
- Open Location

Final pre-submission checklist

  1. Verify the template name and category.
  2. Confirm every variable has a sample.
  3. Replace sample URLs and phone numbers.
  4. Remove guarantees and unsupported urgency.
  5. Check consent and opt-out wording.
  6. Test every button destination.
